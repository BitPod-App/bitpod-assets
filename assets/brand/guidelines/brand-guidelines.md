# BitPod App — Starter Brand Guidelines

## Status
This is a working identity system, not a final canonical brand package.

## Core idea
BitPod App should feel:
- Bitcoin-native
- modern
- product-ready
- clear rather than over-symbolic
- confident without crypto cliché noise

## Primary mark
Use the logo mark when space is limited:
- avatars
- repo icons
- social thumbnails
- compact corners on cards

Preferred file:
- `bitpod-logo-mark-color.svg`

## Lockups
### Horizontal lockup
Best for:
- headers
- landing sections
- README hero areas
- social banners

Preferred file:
- `bitpod-logo-horizontal-color.svg`

### Vertical lockup
Best for:
- splash screens
- centered posters
- stacked brand cards

Preferred file:
- `bitpod-logo-vertical-color.svg`

## Color palette
### Core colors
- Background navy: `#0C122C`
- Warm text / off-white: `#F4EFE8`
- Muted text: `#B9BFCE`
- Accent orange: `#F2912C`

### Gradient range
- Orange: `#FF6A1A`
- Amber: `#FFB12A`
- Magenta: `#D945C1`
- Violet: `#5A2BE7`

## Background usage
### On dark backgrounds
Use:
- color logo
- white logo
- square avatar white

### On light backgrounds
Use:
- black logo
- avoid weak low-contrast placement

## Clear space
Minimum clear space around the logo mark:
- at least the width of the inner dark cut shape on all sides

## Minimum size
Recommended minimums:
- Mark only: 24 px tall digital minimum
- Horizontal lockup: 120 px wide digital minimum
- Vertical lockup: 140 px tall digital minimum

## Do
- keep backgrounds clean
- prefer dark backgrounds for the color mark
- use one logo version at a time
- keep the mark large enough to breathe

## Do not
- add extra gradients
- rotate or skew the mark
- outline it
- place it over noisy images without a panel
- turn it into a coin icon or bolt extra crypto symbols onto it

## Starter note
This package is intentionally lean.
Replace or evolve it later if the final BitPod identity moves in a different direction.
